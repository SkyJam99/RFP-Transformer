Project Background and Purpose:

The purpose of this RFP is to provide proposers with sufficient information to enable them to prepare and submit a proposal for consideration by the City in the acquisition of a software platform for communicating with existing and planned ITS devices. The City is seeking to purchase a software platform to integrate inputs and outputs for its existing and planned ITS devices into a common platform.  In general, the City is interested in off-the-shelf platforms that will require minimal customization.  The City will not reimburse any costs incurred in responding to this RFP.  The consultant should be able to provide all of the services outlined in the following Project Scope Components section. 

 

Project Scope Components:

Device Integration

The primary focus of this RFP is the following ITS devices:

•       22 Acyclica Road Trends

•       2 EDI DA-300s (Acyclica)

•       10 EDI DA-400s (Acyclica) o All of these devices are directly connected to the City’s network.  Acyclica (now owned by EDI) has an API available.

•       6 Vaisala RWIS o These devices are not currently connected to the City’s network but can be with a minimal amount of effort.  They currently communicate directly to Vaisala via cell modem.

•       2 (mounted back-to-back) Daktronic DMS o NTCIP 1203 v. 3 compliant

•       17 Wavetronix HD Sidefire Permanent Count Stations o All of these devices are directly connected to the City’s network.

•       1 Trafficware ATMS Central Software System communicating in turn to approximately 95 traffic signals.

o Trafficware is releasing a new TMDD module for ATMS that utilizes the TMDD 3.03d standard for center-to-center communications.  If proposers wish to utilize this module, they should indicate such in their proposal but not include any procurement costs as the City will procure the module directly from Trafficware.

 

Of secondary or future interest would be incorporating the following devices:

•       1 Trafficware Pod Permanent Count Station

•       58 Panasonic PTZ cameras o These devices are directly connected to the City’s network.  The City’s current software platform is ASM300, utilizing duplicate recorders located at the MOC and Police Dept.  The City understands that ASM300 is likely to be deprecated soon and is independently pursuing an alternate camera platform.

•       67 RTC Connect School Flashers o These devices are remotely accessible via cell modems utilizing the proprietary RTC Connect application.

•       7 permanently mounted radar feedback signs – these are of several manufacturers and currently lack communications.  Primary use case for these would be a future CV Curve Speed Warning application.

•       14 Tapco RRFBs – these currently lack communications.  Primary use case would be for a future CV Pedestrian Warning application. 

 

Desired Features

•       Ability to poll status of all connected devices

•       Ability to use status of one device to change operation of another (e.g. automatically post travel time data to a DMS or change timing plans based on weather information). 

Such ability shall be fully configurable by users with appropriate permissions.

•       Ability to automatically ingest data from commercial providers, such as Waze.

•       Display device status and information utilizing both user configurable graphical and dashboard displays.

•       Support multiple user permission levels

•       Allow pre-configured actions to be manually implemented (e.g. change timing plans and activate a predetermined DMS message as a single action)

•       Generate manual and automatic reports of both system status (device uptime, etc.) and system performance measures (congestion indices, travel times, etc.)

•       Send out pre-configured alerts via email, text or social media, either manually or in response to triggers

•       Ability to send and receive information to and from connected vehicles, or at a minimum given current uncertainties, an identified path to add such capabilities in the near future.

•       Ability for users with appropriate permissions to remotely monitor and initiate actions without being physically present at the TOC.

•       Training on system setup, usage and device management.

 

City Provided Equipment and Software The City will provide:

•       All ITS Equipment

•       All communications to/from field devices

•       Any required servers, including operating system and standard software, such as SQL.  

•       Trafficware ATMS Central Software System, including the TMDD module if needed.  The intent is to continue to use the ATMS software to communicate with the individual traffic signals, including uploading/downloading, programming, field alarms, etc.
